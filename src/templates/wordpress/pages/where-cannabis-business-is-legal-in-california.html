Why cannabis business is not legal everywhere

The state allows cannabis business activities, which include:

  • Cultivation
  • Manufacturing
  • Testing laboratories
  • Distribution
  • Retail

But each city or county can determine whether they allow these activities in their area.

They can:

  • Allow all activities
  • Allow activities, but set limits on them
  • Allow some activities and prohibit others
  • Prohibit all activities

The majority of counties and cities prohibit all cannabis business activities.

Some cities and counties allow an activity, but place restrictions or set limits on it. For example, they may limit the number of licenses in their area. Due to these limits, there are only 1,504 retail licenses in California, or 3.8 retail licenses per 100K people.

If a county has its own rules, those rules only apply to areas in the county that are not incorporated. These are areas that do not have a council or other form of local government in place.

If a city or county does not make their own rules, then their area follows state rules.

Despite cannabis being legal, 2/3 of cities and counties prohibit any retail activity. The majority of Californians cannot:

  • Buy it legally where they live, even for medical use
  • Get safe, tested products in their area

People in areas that prohibit retail activity may turn to the illegal market to meet their needs. As a result, the illegal market continues to thrive. And licensed businesses struggle to stay afloat amid competition from the illegal market.
