Cannabis is legal in California. But cities and counties can prohibit cannabis businesses, like retail. Varying and inconsistent laws cause confusion for consumers and challenges for business owners. And it makes it harder for people to get safe and legal cannabis products.

Why cannabis businesses are not allowed everywhere

Cannabis businesses are regulated in two ways:

  • By the state, and
  • By their city and county.

The state issues licenses based on the type of activity that a business performs. This includes:

  • Cultivation
  • Manufacturing
  • Testing laboratories
  • Distribution
  • Retail
  • Microbusinesses

But each city or county can decide whether to license cannabis businesses in their area. They can:

  • License all cannabis businesses
  • License some types and prohibit others
  • Prohibit all cannabis businesses

Why cities may have different rules than their county

Counties can decide which types of cannabis businesses they license or prohibit. But county rules only apply to the unincorporated parts of the county. They do not apply to incorporated cities that are within the county. 

Incorporated cities can decide which types they license or prohibit within their city. Their rules do not need to align with the rules of the county where they’re located.

More limits on cannabis businesses

Some cities and counties license a type of cannabis business, but also put restrictions on it. For example, they may limit the number of businesses that can get a license. 

Due to these limits, there are less than 4 retail licenses per 100K people in California.

Benefits of cannabis businesses for Californians

About 55% of Californians can buy products from a licensed cannabis retailer.

Products sold by licensed cannabis retailers:

  • Must meet strict testing and safety requirements, and
  • Face marketing controls designed to protect youth.

Licensed cannabis businesses bring many benefits to their communities, including:

  • Job creation
  • Economic development
  • Pathways for legacy operators to move into the legal market
